Plan Focus

Swim is your limiter. Your current 2:30/100m easy pace shows solid potential, and your Olympic swim of ~31 min for 1500m confirms you can sustain effort in the water. But a 70.3 swim is 400m longer and in open water. This plan gives swimming 3 sessions/week in the base phase, including a CSS test in Week 1 to establish your actual threshold pace, open water practice from Week 8, and sighting drills throughout. Bike is your engine. You are already riding 150 km/week at 33 km/h avg — a strong foundation. We build from your current 3-hour max to a peak long ride of 3.5 hours with race-pace blocks and cadence work. Run is your weapon. A 45-min 10K off the bike in your Olympic tri is exceptional for your experience level. But we must manage run volume carefully because your soccer schedule adds ~4 hours/week of running load on Tuesday, Thursday, and Saturday. This plan accounts for that. Nutrition is the fourth discipline. You have never fuelled during racing. Gut training starts in Week 9, building from 40 g/hr to 70–80 g/hr by race day.

Tuesday and Thursday afternoons are blocked for soccer training. Saturday is game day + recovery. All triathlon sessions on those days are morning-only (before 8 AM or evening if morning is missed). Sunday has no time limit and is your primary long session day.

How To Use This Plan
  1. Read the full week before you start it. Your schedule weaves triathlon around soccer — plan pool access, bike routes, and run sessions at the start of each week so nothing clashes.
  2. Train by HR and pace, not just feel. This plan gives you specific heart rate zones and pace targets for every session. Use your GPS watch and HR monitor. If your HR is above the prescribed zone, slow down — ego is the enemy of aerobic development.
  3. Complete every recovery week as written (Weeks 4, 8, 12, 16, 20). You love intensity and hate rest — but adaptation happens during recovery, not during the session. Skipping recovery is the fastest way to plateau or get injured. Rest is performance-enhancing, not laziness.
  4. Soccer counts as training load. Tuesday and Thursday afternoons add ~4 hours of running per week to your body. This plan accounts for that — do not add extra run sessions on soccer days.
  5. Practise nutrition on the bike from Week 9. You have never fuelled during racing. Your gut needs 10–12 weeks of progressive training to absorb 70–80 g/hr of carbohydrates without GI distress. Start at 40 g/hr and build.
  6. Sleep is your biggest performance gap. You are averaging 6–7 hours. Every additional 30 minutes of sleep measurably improves recovery, HRV, and training adaptation. Aim for 7.5–8 hours. This single change will have more impact than any workout in this plan.
