timeline Your Training Phases

foundation

Base Building Weeks 1–6

Foundation building phase focused on aerobic development and technique refinement. All sessions are in Zone 1-2 (easy, conversational pace). This is where your endurance engine is built through consistent, sustainable training. No racing or high intensity - just patient base building.

trending_up

Strength & Endurance Weeks 7–11

Race-specific fitness development phase. Introduces threshold and race-pace sessions while maintaining aerobic base. Training becomes more specific to your target event demands. Recovery becomes crucial as training stress increases. 1-2 lower priority races permitted.

bolt

Peak Fitness Weeks 12–14

Highest training stress phase with frequent race simulations. Sessions become shorter but more intense, focusing on race-pace execution and mental preparation. Practice race-day logistics, nutrition, and pacing strategies. Maximum fitness development period.

self_improvement

Taper & Race Prep Weeks 15–16

Volume reduction while maintaining intensity to arrive at race day fresh but fit. Reduce training by 20-50% while keeping legs turning over. Focus on race preparation, equipment checks, and positive mental rehearsal. Trust your training - fitness is already built.

Phase 1: Foundation Build

Weeks 1–6

Week 1 — BASE
5.0h 13 Apr–19 2026
Welcome to your 70.3 journey! This first week establishes your aerobic base with gentle technique focus. All sessions should feel comfortable and conversational - resist any urge to go harder.

Tue - Swim Technique (6:00am–6:35am)

SWIM Z1-Z2 35min
Warm-up: 200m easy freestyle, focusing on long, smooth strokes
Main Set: 8 × 50m freestyle (30s rest) - focus on high elbow catch and full extension. 4 × 100m easy freestyle (20s rest) emphasizing bilateral breathing every 3-5 strokes. 6 × 50m freestyle (20s rest) - count strokes per length, aim for consistency
Cool-down: 200m easy freestyle, concentrate on relaxed rhythm
Total: 1200m
Coach's Note:
Technique is your foundation - every stroke counts more than speed right now. Focus on feeling the water with your fingertips and maintaining a high elbow throughout the pull phase. If you're getting breathless, you're going too fast. This should feel sustainable and controlled throughout.

Wed - Bike Endurance (6:00am–6:35am)

BIKE Z1-Z2 35min
Warm-up: 10 min easy spinning at 85-90 RPM, gradually building to comfortable effort
Main Set: 20 min steady Zone 2 effort - you should be able to hold a conversation. Focus on maintaining 85-95 RPM cadence throughout. Practice smooth pedal strokes and relaxed upper body
Cool-down: 5 min easy spinning, allowing heart rate to drop gradually
Total: 35 min
Coach's Note:
Higher cadence is crucial for preserving your legs for the run in triathlon. Many beginners default to grinding bigger gears - resist this urge. Your legs should feel smooth and relatively fresh when you finish. If you can't chat comfortably, ease off the intensity.

Thu - Easy Run (6:00am–6:35am)

RUN Z1-Z2 35min
Warm-up: 5 min walk, then 5 min very easy jog building to comfortable pace
Main Set: 20 min steady easy running at conversational pace (around 7:30/km). Focus on landing midfoot with a cadence of 170-180 steps per minute. Keep effort controlled - you should finish feeling like you could continue
Cool-down: 5 min easy walk, gentle stretching of calves and quads
Total: 35 min
Coach's Note:
This easy pace builds your aerobic engine without creating excessive fatigue. Aim for a rhythm where you could easily hold a conversation - if you're breathing hard, slow down. Focus on quick, light steps rather than long strides. Your body is adapting to the training stimulus, so patience with pace is key.

Fri - Long Ride (6:00am–7:15am)

BIKE Z1-Z2 75min
Warm-up: 10 min easy spinning, gradually increasing to comfortable Zone 2 effort
Main Set: 55 min steady endurance riding at 85-95 RPM. Practice nutrition timing - take a small sip every 10-15 minutes. Maintain relaxed shoulders and focus on smooth, circular pedal strokes. Stay in Zone 2 throughout
Cool-down: 10 min easy spinning, allowing effort to drop naturally
Total: 75 min
Coach's Note:
This is your most important weekly bike session - steady endurance builds your aerobic base efficiently. Practice drinking regularly even though you may not feel thirsty. Keep your cadence high to prepare your legs for the run portion of triathlon. The effort should feel sustainable for much longer than 75 minutes.

Sat - Long Run (7:00am–8:35am)

RUN Z1-Z2 95min
Warm-up: 10 min walk/easy jog building to comfortable running pace
Main Set: 75 min continuous running at easy conversational pace (approximately 7:15-7:30/km). Focus on consistent effort rather than pace - adjust for terrain and conditions. Practice relaxed running form with quick cadence. Take walking breaks if needed to maintain conversation pace
Cool-down: 10 min easy walk with gentle stretching
Total: 95 min
Coach's Note:
Your weekly long run is the cornerstone of endurance development - it teaches your body to use fat as fuel efficiently. The pace should allow for complete sentences throughout. Don't worry about speed; focus on time on your feet and developing mental resilience. This effort level builds mitochondria and capillary density that forms your aerobic foundation. Remember to hydrate during longer efforts like this.

Regular position changes and consistent hydration are race-day skills you're developing now. The standing breaks help prevent stiffness and prepare different muscle groups. Your Race Strategy

Swim Strategy

Start wide and back to avoid the washing machine. First 200m: deliberately slow stroke rate, focus on full exhale underwater. Sight every 10-12 strokes using crocodile eyes technique. Find feet to draft behind after 400m. Stay relaxed - you've got two more disciplines to go.

T1 & Bike Strategy

Smooth, unhurried transition. First 10 minutes on bike: settle into race rhythm at 85-95 RPM. Target Zone 2 effort (70-75% perceived) - you should be able to hold a conversation. Begin fuelling at 20 minutes with 30-60g carbs per hour. Save your legs for the run - resist urges to surge on hills.

T2 & Run Strategy

Take time to compose yourself in T2. First 5 minutes running: expect heavy legs, this is normal. Target slightly slower than goal pace initially, then settle into race rhythm. Break the run into segments - aid station to aid station. Fuel every 30-45 minutes if over 90 minutes total race time.

Mental Strategy

Race Day Mantras

'Smooth is fast' - for maintaining efficiency when things get tough
'I've trained for this' - confidence booster during difficult moments
'One segment at a time' - break the race into manageable chunks

Managing Adversity

Expect something to not go perfectly - that's racing. Use the 3 R technique: Rest (let go of what just happened), Regroup (assess current situation), Refocus (next immediate task). Remember: everyone else is suffering too, you're not alone.

Energy Management

Negative split each discipline - start conservatively, finish strong. When you feel good early, resist going harder. When you feel bad, remind yourself it's temporary. Trust your pacing strategy and stick to it regardless of what others are doing.

school Glossary

Zone 2

Aerobic base pace where you can hold a full conversation. About 70-75% of maximum effort. Most training should happen here.

Brick Session

Bike-to-run training with no break between disciplines to practice race transitions and leg adaptation.

CSS (Critical Swim Speed)

Your threshold swimming pace, calculated from 400m and 200m test times. Used to set training zones.

Cadence

Steps per minute (running) or pedal revolutions per minute (cycling). Target 170-180 spm running, 85-95 RPM cycling.

Negative Split

Racing strategy where the second half is faster than the first half. Requires discipline early but leads to stronger finishes.

Transition (T1/T2)

Change areas in triathlon. T1 = swim to bike, T2 = bike to run. Practice smooth, efficient transitions.

Sighting

Looking forward while swimming to navigate. Use 'crocodile eyes' technique - eyes only, not full head lift.

Taper

Training reduction before race day. Decrease volume 20-50% while maintaining intensity to arrive fresh but fit.

Fuelling

Consuming carbohydrates during exercise. Target 30-60g per hour for events 60-90 minutes, 60-80g for longer efforts.

RPE

Rate of Perceived Exertion. Scale of 1-10 for how hard exercise feels. Zone 2 = 4-5/10, race pace = 7-8/10.

Aerobic Base

Your body's ability to use oxygen efficiently. Built through lots of easy-pace training in Zone 1-2.

Strides

Short 20-30 second accelerations to practice good running form and leg turnover. Not sprints - controlled efforts.

Bilateral Breathing

Swimming technique breathing on both sides (every 3 strokes). Improves stroke balance and open water navigation.

Perfect Your Swim Start

Start wide and back in the swim to avoid contact. Seed yourself conservatively - it's better to pass people than be passed over.

Master Race Day Cadence

Practice 85-95 RPM on every bike session. Higher cadence saves your legs for the run by using more cardiovascular than muscular energy.

sports_tennis

Practice Brick Transitions

Do bike-to-run sessions with no break. Your legs will feel like concrete initially - this is normal and improves with practice.

restaurant

Start Fuelling Early

Begin taking in carbs within the first 20-30 minutes of longer sessions. Don't wait until you feel hungry or tired - prevent the bonk before it happens.

psychology

Chunk Your Race

Break the race into small segments - aid station to aid station, or km markers. Focus only on reaching the next checkpoint.

schedule

Trust Your Taper

Feeling sluggish in the final weeks is normal. Your body is absorbing training adaptations. Stick to easy sessions and arrive fresh on race day.

visibility

Master Open Water Sighting

Practice looking forward every 10-12 strokes using just your eyes, not lifting your whole head. Sight off large landmarks, not just buoys.

self_improvement

Negative Split Strategy

Start each discipline 5-10 seconds per km/100m slower than goal pace. Build into race pace, finish strong. Early restraint = late-race strength.
