Focus on technique in all three sports, get comfortable with your morning routine, and build consistency. Remember this is about laying foundations - your soccer training adds significant cardio load already.

Monday - Swim Technique

SWIM Z1-Z2 60min
Warm-up: 400m easy FC (2:20/100m), 200m choice stroke
Technique: 8 x 25m drill (catch-up, fist, fingertip drag) rest 20s
Main Set: 4 x 200m FC @ 2:10/100m, rest 45s
Cool-down: 200m easy choice stroke
Total: 1600m
Coach's Note:
Perfect way to start your triathlon journey with technique focus. The catch-up drill will help establish your bilateral breathing rhythm and stroke timing. Keep the main set steady but controlled - you're building feel for the water, not chasing times yet. Notice how your body position changes with each drill.

bookTraining Glossary

Aerobic Base:

Foundation fitness built through Z1-Z2 training that improves fat burning and endurance capacity.

Brick Session:

Back-to-back bike and run training to practice the transition and adapt to running off the bike.

CSS (Critical Swim Speed):

Your sustainable threshold pace for swimming, typically equivalent to 1500m race pace.

Drafting:

Swimming closely behind another athlete to reduce water resistance and conserve energy.

FTP (Functional Threshold Power):

The highest average power you can sustain for one hour on the bike.

Lactate Threshold:

The intensity where lactate accumulation begins to outpace clearance - roughly Z4 effort.

Negative Split:

Racing strategy where the second half is faster than the first half.

Open Water:

Swimming in natural bodies of water (ocean, lake) rather than pools - requires different skills.

Progressive Build:

Gradually increasing intensity within a set or session, starting easy and finishing strong.

Race Pace:

The target pace/effort you plan to maintain during your race.

RPE (Rate of Perceived Exertion):

Subjective measure of exercise intensity on a scale of 1-10.

Sighting:

Looking up during open water swimming to check direction and navigate the course.

Sweet Spot:

Training zone between Z2 and Z3 (88-93% FTP) that maximizes training adaptations.

Taper:

Planned reduction in training volume before a race to ensure optimal recovery and performance.

Tempo:

Comfortably hard effort (Z3) that you can sustain for 30-60 minutes.

Time Trial (TT):

Solo effort against the clock to test current fitness and pacing.
