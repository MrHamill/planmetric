timeline Your Training Phases

foundation

Base Building Weeks 1–6

Foundation phase focused on building aerobic capacity through high volume, low intensity training. Sessions emphasize Zone 1-2 efforts, technique development, and gradual volume progression. This phase establishes the aerobic engine that supports all future training.

trending_up

Strength & Endurance Weeks 7–13

Race-specific fitness development phase introducing threshold and higher intensity work. Volume continues to increase while adding 1-2 quality sessions per week. Brick sessions become regular, and race-pace efforts are practiced to build specific fitness for your target event.

bolt

Peak Fitness Weeks 14–18

Highest training load phase with maximum race-specific intensity. Sessions include regular race simulations, threshold intervals, and brick combinations. This phase develops peak fitness and race-readiness through carefully managed high-stress training blocks.

self_improvement

Taper & Race Prep Weeks 19–20

Volume reduction phase (30-50%) while maintaining intensity to optimize race-day freshness. Training focuses on maintaining feel and sharpness rather than building fitness. Mental preparation and race strategy refinement become primary focus areas.

Phase 1: Foundation Build

Weeks 1–6

Week 1 — BASE
6.3h 13 Apr–19 2026
Welcome to your Ironman journey! This first week establishes your aerobic base with gentle technique work and conversational-pace sessions. Focus on consistent effort rather than speed - all sessions should feel comfortable and sustainable.

Tue - Swim Technique (6:00am–6:40am)

SWIM Z1-Z2 40min
Warm-up: 200m easy freestyle, focusing on relaxed breathing and long strokes
Main Set: 8 × 50m catch-up drill (pause when hands meet in front) with 15 sec rest, then 8 × 50m fingertip drag drill with 15 sec rest, then 4 × 100m smooth freestyle at CSS +15 sec with 20 sec rest
Cool-down: 200m easy freestyle, focus on high elbow recovery
Total: 1,200m
Coach's Note:
Technique is your foundation - every drill should be performed with precision rather than speed. Focus on feeling the water with your fingertips during the catch phase. If form breaks down, take extra rest between repetitions. This early technique work will pay massive dividends later in your training.

Wed - Bike Endurance (6:00am–6:40am)

BIKE Z1-Z2 40min
Warm-up: 10 min easy spinning at 85-95 RPM, gradually building to Zone 1
Main Set: 25 min steady Zone 2 at 56-75% FTP, maintain 85-100 RPM cadence throughout
Cool-down: 5 min easy spinning, focus on smooth pedal stroke
Total: 40 min
Coach's Note:
Your cadence is crucial for triathlon success - aim for 85-100 RPM to preserve your legs for the run. This higher cadence may feel unnatural initially, but it's more cardiovascular than muscular, leaving you fresher for T2. Stay relaxed in your upper body and focus on smooth, circular pedal strokes.

Thu - Easy Run (6:00am–6:40am)

RUN Z1-Z2 40min
Warm-up: 5 min walk to easy jog, building to Zone 1
Main Set: 30 min conversational pace at 6:00-6:30/km, aim for 170-180 steps per minute
Cool-down: 5 min easy walk
Total: 40 min
Coach's Note:
This should feel genuinely easy - you should be able to hold a full conversation throughout. Focus on quick, light steps with minimal vertical bounce. Your cadence of 170-180 steps per minute reduces injury risk and improves efficiency. If you feel breathless, you're going too fast.

Fri - Swim Threshold (6:00am–6:50am)

SWIM Z3-Z4 50min
Warm-up: 300m easy freestyle building pace every 100m
Main Set: 6 × 100m at CSS pace (your 1:45/100m threshold) with 20 sec rest, then 200m easy recovery, then 4 × 100m at CSS pace with 20 sec rest
Cool-down: 300m easy freestyle, focus on distance per stroke
Total: 1,500m
Coach's Note:
These threshold intervals should feel comfortably hard - sustainable but requiring focus. Maintain stroke count consistency across all 100m repeats, as stroke efficiency matters more than raw speed. Take the prescribed rest fully to maintain quality throughout the set.

Sat - Long Run (7:00am–8:45am)

RUN Z1-Z2 105min
Warm-up: 10 min easy building from walk to Zone 1 pace
Main Set: 85 min steady conversational pace at 6:00-6:30/km, stay in Zone 1-2 throughout
Cool-down: 10 min easy walk with gentle stretching
Total: 105 min (17.5km)
Coach's Note:
Your long run is the cornerstone of Ironman preparation. Stay disciplined with pacing - it should feel almost too easy for the first hour. Hydrate regularly (aim for 500-600ml per hour) and practice your race-day fueling if planning to carry nutrition. This aerobic base building is where your endurance foundation is built.

Sun - Long Ride (7:00am–8:45am)

BIKE Z1-Z2 105min
Warm-up: 15 min easy spinning, building to Zone 1
Main Set: 80 min steady Zone 2 at 56-75% FTP, practice nutrition every 30 min if over 90 min
Cool-down: 10 min easy spinning
Total: 105 min
Coach's Note:
This is prime aerobic base-building territory. Maintain 85-100 RPM cadence and stay relaxed in your upper body. Practice taking one hand off the bars safely to simulate reaching for nutrition. Hydrate consistently (750ml per hour) and if the session exceeds 90 minutes, practice consuming 30-40g carbs per hour to train your gut.

Negative splitting teaches pacing discipline and race-day execution. The first 80 minutes should feel conservative, then gradually build your effort in the final quarter. Those tempo surges teach your body to handle pace changes while maintaining aerobic fitness. Your Race Strategy

Swim Strategy

Start positioning: Position yourself according to your ability - stronger swimmers toward the front/inside, others toward the back/outside to avoid contact.

First 200m: Start 10-15% easier than target pace. Focus on finding clear water and establishing rhythm. Sight every 10-12 strokes using 'crocodile' technique.

Middle section: Settle into race pace, draft when possible (30-50cm behind feet of similar-pace swimmer), maintain steady stroke rate.

Final 200m: Gradually increase stroke rate, prepare for T1 by swimming toward exit point.

Bike Strategy

First 15 minutes: Ease into race effort, allow heart rate to settle. Focus on smooth pedaling at 85-95 RPM.

Main section: Hold 75-80% FTP (avoid going harder early). Begin fueling within first 30 minutes: 60-80g carbs/hour, 500-700mg sodium/hour, 750-1000ml fluid/hour.

Final 30 minutes: Maintain power output, prepare legs for run with slightly higher cadence (90-100 RPM).

Run Strategy

First 2km: Expect heavy legs - this is normal. Start 10-15 seconds/km slower than target pace, focus on quick turnover and forward lean.

Middle section: Build to target pace gradually. Fuel every 30-35 minutes, walk through aid stations if needed for proper fueling.

Final section: Trust your training, maintain form, use mental strategies like chunking (focus only on reaching next aid station).

Mental Strategy

Pre-Race Mental Preparation

Visualization: Spend 10-15 minutes the night before visualizing each race segment going smoothly. See yourself handling challenges calmly and finishing strong.

Process focus: Instead of time goals, focus on process goals: 'smooth stroke rate,' 'steady power output,' 'consistent fueling.'

During Race Mental Tools

Positive self-talk: Prepare 3-4 mantras you've practiced in training: 'Strong and steady,' 'I've trained for this,' 'Smooth is fast,' 'I can do hard things.'

Chunking technique: Break the race into smaller segments - swim to T1, T1 to first bike aid station, etc. Focus only on completing the current chunk.

Transition reset (3 Rs): At each transition: 1) Rest - let go of the previous leg, 2) Regroup - prepare mentally for next discipline, 3) Refocus - narrow attention to immediate next task.

Managing Adversity

'Yes, and...' technique: When things go wrong, acknowledge it and immediately pivot to the positive: 'Yes, that was a rough swim, AND I still have two legs to make up time.'

Reframe difficulty: When suffering: 'This discomfort means I'm working hard and getting closer to the finish.' Every step forward is progress.

Use your training: Remind yourself of all the hard sessions you've completed. You've already proven you can handle discomfort and keep going.

school Glossary

CSS (Critical Swim Speed)

Your sustainable swim pace for longer distances, calculated as (400m time - 200m time) / 2. Used to set training zones and race pace targets.

FTP (Functional Threshold Power)

The highest average power you can sustain for approximately 60 minutes on the bike. All cycling training zones are based on percentages of FTP.

Brick Session

A workout combining bike and run with no rest between disciplines, simulating race transitions and training your body to run efficiently off the bike.

Zone 1-2 (Z1-Z2)

Easy aerobic training intensity where you can hold a conversation. Forms the foundation of endurance training and aids recovery.

Zone 3-4 (Z3-Z4)

Threshold training intensity - comfortably hard effort you can sustain for 20-60 minutes. Builds race-specific fitness and lactate clearance.

Negative Split

Racing strategy where the second half is faster than the first half. Requires disciplined pacing early but typically results in better overall performance.

Sighting

Lifting your head to navigate during open water swimming. Should be done every 10-12 strokes using minimal head movement (eyes only, not full head lift).

Cadence

Pedal revolutions per minute (RPM) in cycling or steps per minute in running. Optimal bike cadence: 85-95 RPM; running: 170-180 steps/minute.

Taper

Training phase before racing where volume decreases 30-50% while maintaining some intensity to optimize freshness and performance on race day.

Transition (T1/T2)

T1: swim-to-bike changeover; T2: bike-to-run changeover. Efficient transitions can save significant time and are often called the 'fourth discipline.'

Aerobic Base

Fundamental endurance fitness built through high volume, low intensity training. Forms the foundation for all other fitness adaptations.

Race Simulation

Training session that mimics race conditions, intensity, nutrition, and transitions to practice race-day execution and identify potential issues.

Fueling Strategy

Plan for consuming carbohydrates, fluids, and electrolytes during racing. Typically 60-80g carbs/hour for events longer than 90 minutes.

Recovery Week

Scheduled reduction in training volume (typically 50-60% of normal) every 3-4 weeks to allow adaptation and prevent overtraining.

Wetsuit Legal

Water temperature conditions (typically 14-22°C) where wetsuits provide advantage through buoyancy and warmth, improving swim efficiency by 2-4 seconds per 100m.

Maintain 85-95 RPM on the bike to preserve your legs for the run. Low cadence (70-75 RPM) burns more glycogen and creates more muscular fatigue, making the run significantly harder.

Practice Race Nutrition

Start fueling within the first 20-30 minutes of any session over 60 minutes. Build tolerance from 40g/hour to 80-90g/hour over 6-7 weeks. Always train with your planned race-day products.

2 sessions per week during base phase, 1 session during build/peak. Focus on single-leg squats, hip stability, and core strength. Heavy compound movements + plyometrics provide the best running economy gains.
