timeline Your Training Phases

foundation

Base Building Weeks 1–6

Foundation phase focused on building aerobic capacity through high-volume, low-intensity training. All sessions in Zone 1-2 with emphasis on technique development, injury prevention, and establishing training consistency. No racing or high-intensity work - we're building the engine that will support later training phases.

trending_up

Strength & Endurance Weeks 7–13

Progressive phase introducing race-specific intensities and longer duration sessions. Combines continued aerobic development with threshold and VO2max work. Lower-priority races acceptable for experience. Training becomes more structured and demanding as we build race-specific fitness on top of your aerobic base.

bolt

Peak Fitness Weeks 14–18

Highest training loads with maximum race-specific preparation. Sessions simulate race conditions with brick workouts, sustained threshold efforts, and maximum weekly volumes. Mental preparation becomes crucial as physical and psychological stress reach their highest levels before taper begins.

self_improvement

Taper & Race Prep Weeks 19–20

Strategic volume reduction (20-50%) while maintaining intensity to optimize race-day performance. Focus shifts to race preparation, equipment finalization, and mental readiness. The goal is to arrive at the start line fresh, confident, and with all fitness adaptations intact.

Phase 1: Foundation Build

Weeks 1–6

Week 1 — BASE
6.6h 13 Apr–19 2026
Welcome to your elite Ironman training journey! This first week establishes your aerobic base across all three disciplines. Focus on technique and consistency - every stroke, pedal turn, and step counts. Keep all efforts conversational and build the foundation for harder training ahead.

Tue - Swim Technique (45 min) (5:30am–6:15am)

SWIM Z1-Z2 45min
Warm-up: 300m easy freestyle, 4 × 50m build (increasing pace each 50m)
Main Set: 8 × 100m @ 1:50/100m (focus on catch and rotation), 50m easy between each. 6 × 50m single-arm drill @ 2:00/100m, alternating arms each 50m. 4 × 100m smooth freestyle @ 1:45/100m
Cool-down: 200m easy choice of stroke
Total: 1800m
Coach's Note:
Foundation week - prioritise stroke technique over speed. Focus on full extension, high elbow catch, and bilateral breathing every 3-5 strokes. Your stroke rate should feel controlled and sustainable. This is about building muscle memory that will serve you in longer sessions ahead.

Wed - Bike Endurance (45 min) (5:30am–6:15am)

BIKE Z1-Z2 45min
Warm-up: 10 min easy spinning at 85-95 RPM
Main Set: 30 min steady endurance at 65-70% FTP, maintaining 90-100 RPM throughout. Focus on smooth pedal stroke and consistent power output
Cool-down: 5 min easy spinning
Total: 45 min
Coach's Note:
Elite-level aerobic base building starts here. Keep power in Zone 2 and maintain that higher cadence - this protects your legs for running later. If you're indoors, ensure good ventilation and have a towel ready. The goal is to feel like you could continue for hours at this effort.

Thu - Easy Run (45 min) (5:30am–6:15am)

RUN Z1-Z2 45min
Warm-up: 10 min easy jog, 4 × 30s strides with walk recovery
Main Set: 30 min continuous running at conversational pace (5:00/km). Focus on 175-180 steps per minute cadence
Cool-down: 5 min easy walk
Total: 45 min
Coach's Note:
Your bread-and-butter easy run. Stay disciplined with pace - you should be able to hold a full conversation throughout. Higher cadence reduces impact forces and builds efficiency. Land midfoot under your center of gravity, not out in front. This effort builds your aerobic engine without accumulating fatigue.

Fri - Swim Threshold (50 min) (5:30am–6:20am)

SWIM Z3-Z4 50min
Warm-up: 400m easy freestyle, 4 × 50m build pace
Main Set: 5 × 200m @ 1:25/100m with 30s rest (threshold effort). 200m easy recovery. 8 × 50m @ 1:20/100m with 15s rest (slightly above threshold)
Cool-down: 250m easy choice
Total: 2000m
Coach's Note:
First threshold session - this should feel comfortably hard, sustainable for the prescribed distance. Breathing will be elevated but controlled. Focus on maintaining stroke length even as effort increases. The 50m set teaches you to hold speed with shorter rest - crucial for race situations where you need to respond to moves or currents.

Sat - Long Run (110 min) (5:30am–7:20am)

RUN Z1-Z2 110min
Warm-up: 15 min easy jog building to steady pace
Main Set: 85 min continuous running at 5:10-5:20/km (slightly slower than easy run pace). Stay relaxed and focus on efficient form
Cool-down: 10 min easy walk with light stretching
Total: 110 min (~22km)
Coach's Note:
Your most important weekly session. This builds the aerobic capacity that makes or breaks Ironman performance. Stay patient with pace - the goal is time on feet, not speed. Hydrate with 150-200ml every 15-20 minutes after the first 30 minutes. Practice your race-day nutrition strategy and take a gel or sports drink at 60 minutes to train your gut.

Sun - Long Ride (110 min) (5:30am–7:20am)

BIKE Z1-Z2 110min
Warm-up: 15 min easy spinning
Main Set: 85 min steady aerobic riding at 65-75% FTP, maintaining 85-95 RPM. Include 4 × 30s efforts at 90 RPM every 20 minutes to practice leg speed
Cool-down: 10 min easy spinning
Total: 110 min
Coach's Note:
Aerobic base building at its finest. This effort should feel sustainable for much longer - you're building the engine that will carry you 180km on race day. Hydrate 500-750ml per hour and practice your bike nutrition with sports drink or diluted energy drink. Keep power steady and resist the urge to surge on hills or into headwinds.

Your Race Strategy

Swim Strategy

Start conservatively in first 200m - find clear water and establish rhythm. Sight every 10-12 strokes using 'crocodile' technique. Draft when possible (30-50cm behind feet) to save 20-30% energy. Focus on long strokes and bilateral breathing.

T1 Strategy

Jog to bike, not sprint. Sit to put on shoes and helmet. Leave wetsuit at feet, don't carry. Check gears before mounting line.

Bike Strategy

First 15 minutes at 5-10 watts below target to settle. Maintain 85-95 RPM throughout. Begin fuelling within first 30 minutes: 70-80g carbs/hour + 600-800ml fluid/hour. Hold back 5% effort for final third - the bike sets up the run.

T2 Strategy

Rack bike properly, change shoes sitting down, grab run nutrition. Brief 30-second mental reset: 'I've trained for this, time to run strong.'

Run Strategy

First 2km slightly slower than target pace to establish rhythm. Fuel every 25-30 minutes with practiced nutrition. Break race into segments: aid station to aid station. Negative split: controlled first half, progressively stronger second half.

Mental Strategy

Pre-Race Mental Prep

Visualize your perfect race the night before. Review your pacing strategy and nutrition plan. Accept that some discomfort is normal - you've trained to handle it. Focus on process goals, not just time goals.

During Race Mental Tools

Chunking: Break the race into smaller segments. Focus only on reaching the next aid station or kilometer marker. Positive Self-Talk: Use rehearsed mantras: 'Strong and steady', 'I've done this in training', 'Smooth is fast'. 3 Rs at Transitions: Rest (let go of previous leg), Regroup (prepare for next discipline), Refocus (narrow attention to immediate task).

Managing Adversity

Expect something to go wrong - it's normal in racing. Practice 'Yes, and...' thinking: 'Yes, that was tough, and I still have two legs to race well.' Stay present-focused rather than projecting negative outcomes. Remember: everyone else is suffering too - your training gives you the edge.

FTP

Functional Threshold Power - the highest power you can sustain for 60 minutes, measured in watts

Brick Session

Bike-to-run workout with no break between disciplines to practice race transitions and leg adaptation

Z1-Z2

Zone 1-2 training intensity - easy, conversational pace below lactate threshold (aerobic base building)

Z3-Z4

Zone 3-4 training intensity - threshold/tempo effort at or slightly above lactate threshold

RPM

Revolutions Per Minute - cycling cadence, optimal range 85-95 RPM for triathletes

Negative Split

Racing strategy where second half is faster than first half, requires discipline early in race

Sighting

Looking forward while swimming to navigate in open water, should be done every 10-12 strokes

Lactate Threshold

Exercise intensity where lactate accumulates faster than it can be cleared, sustainable for ~60 minutes

Taper

Planned reduction in training volume (20-50%) while maintaining intensity before race day

TSS

Training Stress Score - metric quantifying workout difficulty based on intensity and duration

VO2max

Maximum oxygen consumption rate - Zone 5 training typically at 106-120% FTP

Drafting

Swimming 30-50cm behind another athlete's feet to reduce energy expenditure by 20-30%

Periodisation

Systematic training plan progression through Base, Build, Peak, and Taper phases

Race Pace

Target intensity you can sustain for full race distance based on current fitness level

pool

Master Open Water Skills

Practice sighting every 10-12 strokes and bilateral breathing. Open water swimming costs 15-20% more energy than pool swimming - get specific practice before race day.

speed

High Cadence Cycling

Maintain 85-95 RPM to preserve running legs. Lower cadence burns more glycogen and creates more muscular fatigue, compromising your run performance.

nutrition

Practice Race Nutrition

Never try anything new on race day. Practice fuelling with 70-80g carbs/hour on long rides and runs, starting within the first 30 minutes of exercise.

psychology

Break Race Into Chunks

Don't think about the full distance. Focus on reaching the next aid station, transition, or kilometer marker. Make each segment a mini-race with its own goal.

schedule

Respect Recovery Weeks

Adaptation happens during rest, not during training. Recovery weeks at 50-60% volume every 4th week are mandatory, not optional.

sports_score

Negative Split Strategy

Start conservatively and build throughout the race. Most age-groupers go too hard early - discipline in the first half enables a strong finish.

favorite

80/20 Rule is Sacred

80% of training should be easy (conversational pace), 20% hard. Most athletes train moderate pace too often - polarize your intensity distribution.

directions_run

Protect Your Run Training

Most race time is lost on the run. Never do back-to-back hard run sessions, and prioritize run recovery over other disciplines when fatigued.
