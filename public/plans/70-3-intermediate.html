timeline Your Training Phases

foundation

Base Building Weeks 1–6

Foundation building phase focusing on aerobic development and technique refinement. All training stays in Zone 1-2 with emphasis on consistency, form, and gradually building weekly volume. This is where your aerobic engine develops.

trending_up

Strength & Endurance Weeks 7–11

Structured intensity phase introducing threshold and race-specific sessions. Training becomes more specific to your event demands while maintaining the 80/20 intensity distribution. Fitness gains accelerate through controlled stress and recovery.

bolt

Peak Fitness Weeks 12–14

Highest training load phase with race simulations every 3-4 days. Sessions get progressively shorter but maintain intensity. Your body adapts to handle race-day demands while building confidence through specificity.

self_improvement

Taper & Race Prep Weeks 15–16

Strategic volume reduction (20-50%) while maintaining intensity to arrive race-day fresh but sharp. Your fitness is locked in - this phase optimizes your power-to-weight ratio and ensures full glycogen storage and muscle repair.

Welcome to your 70.3 training journey! This first week establishes the foundation with easy-paced sessions and proper technique focus. All sessions should feel comfortable and conversational, building your aerobic base without stress.

Tue - Swim Technique (6:00am–6:35am)

SWIM Z1-Z2 35min
Warm-up: 200m easy freestyle, 4 × 50m kick with 15 sec rest
Main Set: 6 × 100m freestyle focusing on catch and pull technique with 20 sec rest, 4 × 50m single-arm drill with 15 sec rest, 200m easy build by 50m
Cool-down: 150m easy freestyle
Total: 35 minutes (1,100m)
Coach's Note:
Focus on long, smooth strokes and proper breathing technique throughout. Your catch phase is crucial - imagine grabbing water and pulling yourself over it rather than pushing water backward. Keep your head position neutral and exhale fully underwater to maintain rhythm and reduce anxiety.

BIKE Z1-Z2 35min
Warm-up: 10 min easy spinning at 85-95 RPM
Main Set: 20 min steady Zone 1-2 at 85-100 RPM, focusing on smooth pedal stroke

Total: 35 minutes
Coach's Note:
This is all about establishing proper cadence habits early. Keep your RPM between 85-100 throughout - this higher cadence will save your legs for the run in races. The effort should feel very comfortable and you should be able to hold a full conversation. Use this time to dial in your bike position and get comfortable on the aerobars if you have them.

Thu - Easy Run (6:00am–6:35am)

school Glossary

CSS (Critical Swim Speed)

Your threshold swim pace calculated as (400m time - 200m time) ÷ 2. The pace you can sustain for ~30-40 minutes.

FTP (Functional Threshold Power)

The highest power you can sustain for 1 hour on the bike, measured in watts. Used to set training zones.

Zone 1-2 (Z1-Z2)

Easy aerobic training intensity. Fully conversational pace, 65-75% max heart rate. Forms 80% of total training.

Zone 3-4 (Z3-Z4)

Threshold/tempo intensity. Comfortably hard, sustainable for 20-60 minutes. 85-95% max heart rate.

RPM (Revolutions Per Minute)

Bike cadence - how fast you turn the pedals. Optimal range 85-100 RPM for triathletes to preserve run legs.

Brick Session

Bike immediately followed by run with no rest between. Trains the bike-to-run transition feeling.

Negative Split

Running the second half of a race faster than the first half. Optimal pacing strategy for most distances.

Sighting

Looking forward while swimming to navigate in open water. Every 10-12 strokes using 'crocodile' eyes-only technique.

Cadence

Steps per minute while running. Optimal range 170-180 spm reduces injury risk and improves efficiency.

Taper

Planned reduction in training volume (20-50%) while maintaining intensity in final 1-2 weeks before race.

T1/T2

Transition 1 (swim to bike) and Transition 2 (bike to run). Practice speeds up race day transitions.

Wetsuit Legal

Water temperature 14-22°C where wetsuits are permitted. Provides 2-4 sec/100m advantage through buoyancy.

Fuelling

In-race carbohydrate intake. Target 60-80g/hour for 70.3 distance, starting within first 20-30 minutes.

Draft

Swimming 30-50cm behind another swimmer's feet. Saves 20-30% energy - legal in triathlon swimming.

Build

Gradual increase in pace/effort within a swim length or run interval. Teaches pace control and finishing strong.
