timeline Your Training Phases

foundation

Base Building Weeks 1–4

Foundation phase focusing on building aerobic fitness through high-volume, low-intensity training. All sessions are in Zone 1-2 with emphasis on technique development and establishing consistent training habits. No racing or high-intensity work - this phase builds your engine.

trending_up

Strength & Endurance Weeks 5–8

Progressive phase introducing race-specific intensity and duration. Training becomes more structured with threshold work, tempo sessions, and initial brick workouts. Volume continues to increase while maintaining the 80/20 easy/hard distribution. Low-priority races may be included for experience.

bolt

Peak Fitness Weeks 9–11

Highest training load phase combining maximum volume with race-specific intensity. Sessions simulate race conditions through longer brick workouts, open water swimming, and sustained efforts at goal pace. This phase builds both physical and mental race readiness.

self_improvement

Taper & Race Prep Weeks 12–12

Strategic reduction in training volume (40-60%) while maintaining intensity and movement quality. The goal is to shed accumulated fatigue while preserving fitness gains. Focus shifts to race preparation, equipment checks, and mental readiness rather than fitness building.

Phase 1: Foundation Build

Weeks 1–4

Week 1 — BASE
4.4h 13 Apr–19 2026
Welcome to your Olympic triathlon journey! This first week focuses on establishing good training habits and building your aerobic base. All sessions should feel conversational and comfortable - resist the urge to go hard. Focus on technique over speed, especially in the pool.

Tue - Swim Technique (6:00am–6:35am)

SWIM Z1-Z2 35min
Warm-up: 200m easy freestyle, focusing on relaxed breathing
Main Set: 8 × 50m freestyle with 20 sec rest (focus on high elbow catch), 6 × 50m with 15 sec rest (focus on full extension), 300m easy pull with fins if available
Cool-down: 100m easy backstroke or freestyle
Total: 1200m
Coach's Note:
This is all about building good freestyle habits. Keep your stroke long and relaxed - imagine reaching over a barrel with each stroke. If you feel breathless, you're going too fast. Practice bilateral breathing (every 3rd stroke) during the easy sections to develop balance and rhythm.

Wed - Bike Endurance (6:00am–6:35am)

BIKE Z1-Z2 35min
Warm-up: 5 min easy spinning at high cadence (90+ RPM)
Main Set: 25 min steady endurance pace, focus on maintaining 85-95 RPM cadence throughout
Cool-down: 5 min easy spinning
Total: 35 min
Coach's Note:
Your legs should feel comfortable and you should be able to hold a conversation throughout. Focus on smooth, circular pedaling and higher cadence - this will save your legs for the run in races. If you find yourself grinding big gears at low RPM, shift to an easier gear and spin faster.

Thu - Easy Run (6:00am–6:35am)

RUN Z1-Z2 35min
Warm-up: 5 min walk/easy jog
Main Set: 25 min easy running at conversational pace, aim for 170-180 steps per minute
Cool-down: 5 min walk
Total: 35 min
Coach's Note:
This should feel genuinely easy - you should be able to chat comfortably throughout. Focus on landing midfoot with a slight forward lean from your ankles. Count your steps for 15 seconds and multiply by 4 to check your cadence - aim for 170-180 steps per minute to reduce injury risk.

Fri - Long Ride (6:00am–7:15am)

BIKE Z1-Z2 75min
Warm-up: 10 min easy spinning, gradually building cadence to 85+ RPM
Main Set: 55 min steady endurance riding, maintain 85-95 RPM cadence, practice drinking every 15-20 minutes
Cool-down: 10 min easy spinning
Total: 75 min
Coach's Note:
This is your key aerobic development session for the bike. Stay in an easy gear and focus on smooth, efficient pedaling. Practice your hydration strategy - aim to drink 500-600ml per hour. Your effort should feel sustainable for hours, not just the session duration. Remember: easy days must be genuinely easy to allow for quality work later in the plan.

Sat - Long Run (7:00am–8:25am)

RUN Z1-Z2 85min
Warm-up: 10 min easy jog/walk intervals
Main Set: 65 min easy running at conversational pace, walk for 1-2 minutes every 20 minutes if needed
Cool-down: 10 min walk
Total: 85 min (~10km)
Coach's Note:
Your first long run - this builds your aerobic engine and mental resilience. The key is to finish feeling like you could continue, not exhausted. Walk breaks are perfectly fine and actually beneficial for beginners. Focus on maintaining good form throughout and practice your fueling strategy by taking small sips of water every 15-20 minutes. Listen to your body and don't hesitate to walk if needed.
Week 2 — BASE
5.0h 20 Apr–26 2026
Building on week 1 with slightly increased duration. Continue focusing on technique and aerobic base development. You should be settling into a rhythm with your training schedule. All sessions remain conversational effort - patience now pays dividends later.

Tue - Swim Technique (6:00am–6:40am)

SWIM Z1-Z2 40min
Warm-up: 250m easy freestyle with focus on relaxed breathing pattern
Main Set: 10 × 50m freestyle with 20 sec rest (alternate focus: catch, rotation), 4 × 100m with 30 sec rest (build stroke length each 25m), 200m easy choice stroke
Cool-down: 150m easy backstroke or easy freestyle
Total: 1400m
Coach's Note:
Continue building your stroke efficiency. During the 100m repeats, focus on taking fewer strokes per length - aim to increase your distance per stroke rather than your stroke rate. This builds the efficiency that will save energy during long races. Remember to exhale fully underwater and stay relaxed through your shoulders and arms.

Wed - Bike Endurance (6:00am–6:40am)

BIKE Z1-Z2 40min
Warm-up: 5 min easy spinning, building to comfortable endurance pace
Main Set: 30 min steady endurance pace, continue working on 85-95 RPM cadence, practice shifting smoothly
Cool-down: 5 min easy recovery spinning
Total: 40 min
Coach's Note:
You should be finding your natural rhythm on the bike now. Focus on smooth gear changes and maintaining that higher cadence even as the session gets slightly longer. Your breathing should remain comfortable throughout - if you're working too hard, ease back. This endurance work is building the foundation for all future intensity.

Thu - Easy Run (6:00am–6:40am)

RUN Z1-Z2 40min
Warm-up: 5 min walk/easy jog progression
Main Set: 30 min easy running, focus on midfoot landing and relaxed shoulders
Cool-down: 5 min walk with light stretching
Total: 40 min
Coach's Note:
As the duration increases, form becomes even more important. Focus on running tall with a slight forward lean from your ankles, not your waist. Your arms should swing naturally at your sides - avoid crossing them over your body. If you feel any discomfort, slow down or take walking breaks. Building durability is more important than speed at this stage.

Fri - Long Ride (6:00am–7:15am)

BIKE Z1-Z2 75min
Warm-up: 10 min progressive warm-up, finishing at endurance pace
Main Set: 55 min steady endurance riding, practice eating/drinking every 20 minutes, maintain cadence focus
Cool-down: 10 min easy spinning
Total: 75 min
Coach's Note:
Same duration as last week but your body should be adapting already. This is a great session to practice your race-day nutrition and hydration habits. Try consuming 30-40g of carbohydrates per hour through sports drink or food. Stay relaxed in your upper body and keep that cadence smooth and high. The effort should feel sustainable and controlled throughout.

Sat - Long Run (7:00am–8:35am)

RUN Z1-Z2 95min
Warm-up: 10 min progressive warm-up from walk to easy jog
Main Set: 75 min easy running with optional 2-minute walk breaks every 25 minutes
Cool-down: 10 min walk and light stretching
Total: 95 min (~11-12km)
Coach's Note:
A step up in duration that builds both physical and mental endurance. The key is finishing strong rather than struggling through the final 20 minutes. Practice taking in fluids every 15-20 minutes, especially if it's warm. Your conversational pace might be slightly slower as you adapt to the longer duration - that's completely normal and beneficial for building your aerobic base.
Week 3 — BASE
5.8h 27 Apr–3 May 2026
Your longest week in the base phase. Focus remains on aerobic development and technique refinement. You should be feeling more comfortable with the training rhythm. Continue to prioritize consistency over intensity - every session should still feel controlled and sustainable.

Coach's Note:
As distances increase, maintaining good technique becomes more challenging but more important. Focus on rolling your body as one unit during the longer 100m repeats - your hips and shoulders should move together. During the build 50s, resist the urge to increase stroke rate; instead, apply more pressure to the water with each stroke while maintaining your technique.

With increased duration, you'll really start to feel the aerobic benefits building. At this duration, good running form becomes crucial for injury prevention and efficiency. Every 10 minutes, run through your form checklist: tall posture, midfoot landing, 180 steps per minute, relaxed shoulders, and arms swinging naturally. If your form starts to break down, take a short walk break to reset. Quality movement patterns are more important than covering distance.

Coach's Note:
Same duration as previous weeks, but you should notice improved comfort and efficiency. If you're riding a road bike, spend some time in your aero position (if you have one) to build familiarity, but prioritize comfort and control. Continue practicing your fueling strategy - your body should be adapting to taking in carbohydrates while exercising. Stay patient with the aerobic pace; this base work is essential for everything that comes later.

Sat - Long Run (7:00am–8:50am)

Cool-down: 10 min walk with stretching routine

Coach's Note:
Your longest run yet - a significant step in building endurance. The goal is to finish feeling controlled, not exhausted. Walk breaks are not just allowed but encouraged if they help you maintain good form and conversational effort. For hydration, aim for 150-200ml every 15-20 minutes. This session is as much about mental development as physical - you're proving to yourself that you can handle longer distances with patience and smart pacing.

Phase 2: Aerobic Development

Weeks 5–8

Week 5 — BUILD
6.4h 11 May–17 2026
Welcome to the build phase! We're introducing some targeted intensity work while maintaining your aerobic base. The brick session combines bike and run efforts to practice race-day transitions. Quality over quantity is key - nail the prescribed efforts and keep easy days truly easy.

Tue - Swim Technique (6:00am–6:45am)

SWIM Z1-Z2 45min
Warm-up: 300m easy freestyle, building stroke rate gradually
Main Set: 5 × 100m freestyle with 20 sec rest (steady effort, focus on consistent pacing), 6 × 50m with 15 sec rest (slight build in effort every 2 × 50m), 4 × 50m easy recovery, 200m continuous easy swimming
Cool-down: 200m easy backstroke or choice
Total: 1600m
Coach's Note:
We're starting to add some structured pace work while maintaining technique focus. During the 100m repeats, aim for consistent split times - this teaches you to pace sustained efforts. The building 50s introduce controlled speed increases. Remember, in swimming, smooth is fast. Focus on maintaining your technique even as effort increases, and breathe comfortably throughout.

Wed - Brick Session (6:00am–7:15am)

BRICK Z2-Z3 75min
Warm-up: 10 min easy bike spinning + 5 min easy running
Main Set: 35 min bike at steady tempo effort (comfortably hard, breathing deeper but controlled), immediately into 20 min run at moderate effort (slightly faster than easy pace), no rest between bike and run
Cool-down: 5 min easy walking
Total: 75 min
Coach's Note:
Your first brick session! This teaches your legs to adapt from cycling to running, crucial for triathlon success. The bike effort should feel sustainably hard - you're working but could maintain this for an hour. Transition immediately to the run with heavy legs initially - this is normal and will improve with practice. The run should feel controlled once you settle in. Stay hydrated throughout this longer session, aiming for 500-600ml per hour.

Thu - Easy Run (6:00am–6:45am)

RUN Z1-Z2 45min
Warm-up: 5 min walk to easy jog progression
Main Set: 35 min easy conversational running, focus on recovery from yesterday's brick session
Cool-down: 5 min walk with light stretching
Total: 45 min
Coach's Note:
This recovery run after your first brick session is crucial for adaptation. Your legs might feel heavy initially - that's normal after the bike-run combination. Start even easier than usual and let your body warm up gradually. This session promotes blood flow and recovery while maintaining your running rhythm. Focus on smooth form and don't worry about pace - active recovery is the goal.

Fri - Long Ride (6:00am–7:15am)

BIKE Z1-Z2 75min
Warm-up: 10 min progressive warm-up to endurance pace
Main Set: 55 min steady endurance riding, include 3 × 5 min tempo efforts (comfortably hard) with 5 min easy recovery between
Cool-down: 10 min easy spinning
Total: 75 min
Coach's Note:
We're adding some tempo intervals to develop your threshold power and race-specific fitness. The tempo efforts should feel comfortably hard - you're breathing deeper but still in control. Focus on smooth power delivery and maintaining good form even when working harder. Between intervals, spin easy to recover fully. This session builds the power you'll need for race day while maintaining your aerobic base. Stay hydrated throughout the longer session.

Sat - Long Run (7:00am–8:45am)

RUN Z1-Z2 105min
Warm-up: 10 min progressive walk to steady jog
Main Set: 85 min easy running with 3 × 10 min tempo sections (comfortably hard effort) at 20, 40, and 60 minutes, with 10 min easy between each
Cool-down: 10 min walk with thorough stretching
Total: 105 min (~12-13km)
Coach's Note:
Your longest run with structured tempo work. The tempo sections should feel comfortably hard - faster than easy pace but not racing speed. You should be able to speak in short sentences during these efforts. This develops your lactate threshold and race-specific endurance. Take hydration every 15-20 minutes, especially during the tempo sections. The easy running between and after tempo sections is just as important as the work intervals themselves.

flag Race Day Protocol

Pre-Race Timeline
3-4 hours before: High-carb breakfast (oatmeal, banana, toast with honey). Hydrate with 500ml sports drink.
2 hours before: Arrive at race venue. Set up transition area methodically. Check bike, tire pressure, and all equipment twice.
90 minutes before: Begin sipping sports drink (200-300ml total). Use bathroom. Apply sunscreen and body glide/anti-chafe.
45-60 minutes before: Dynamic warm-up: 5 min easy jog, leg swings, arm circles. Optional: 1 gel with water if race >90 minutes.
30 minutes before: Wetsuit on (if required). Easy swim warm-up: 200m easy with 4 x 25m build. Practice sighting.
15 minutes before: Final equipment check. Mental rehearsal of race plan. Get to swim start position.
5 minutes before: Deep breathing. Positive self-talk: 'I'm ready, I'm trained, I belong here.'
Your Race Strategy

SWIM (1500m): Start conservative - first 200m at easy effort to settle nerves. Sight every 10-12 strokes using 'crocodile eyes' technique. Find feet to draft behind if possible (saves 20-30% energy). Stay relaxed and breathe fully - exhale completely underwater. Target: smooth, sustainable pace around 2:25-2:35/100m.

T1: Take your time - 30 seconds saved here isn't worth a mistake. Dry feet if wet, helmet on and clipped before touching bike. Practice your T1 routine beforehand.

BIKE (40km): First 10 minutes: settle into race position and find rhythm. Target 85-95 RPM cadence throughout. Stay in Zone 2-3 (you should be able to say short sentences). Fuel: 1 gel at 45 minutes if using. Hydrate 150-200ml every 15-20 minutes. Save legs for the run - resist urge to push hard on bike.

T2: Smooth dismount, rack bike securely. Helmet off before touching run gear. Take 10 seconds to compose yourself mentally.

RUN (10km): First 2km: find your rhythm, don't chase early leaders. Middle 6km: settle into race pace, focus on smooth cadence (170-180 spm). Final 2km: This is where your training pays off - stay strong and push when others fade. Use 3-2-1 breathing if you get tight.

Mental Strategy

CHUNKING: Break the race into smaller pieces: swim to T1, bike first half, bike second half, T2, run first 5km, run final 5km. Each segment gets its own focus and mini-celebration when complete.

MANTRAS: Have 2-3 phrases ready: 'Smooth and strong', 'I've trained for this', 'I can do hard things'. Repeat them during difficult moments rather than focusing on discomfort.

3 Rs TECHNIQUE: At each transition - Rest (let go of the last segment), Regroup (prepare mentally for what's next), Refocus (concentrate on the immediate next task). Don't carry mistakes or frustrations between disciplines.

POSITIVE REFRAMING: Nerves = readiness ('I'm excited' not 'I'm nervous'). Discomfort = strength building ('This is where I get stronger'). Use 'Yes, and...' thinking: 'Yes this is hard, AND I'm handling it well.'

PROCESS FOCUS: When the going gets tough, focus on technique - swim stroke rate, bike cadence, run form. This gives your mind something productive to do instead of dwelling on discomfort.

school Glossary

CSS (Critical Swim Speed)

Your threshold swimming pace, calculated as (400m time - 200m time) ÷ 2. Used to set training zones and target race pace.

Zone 1-2 (Easy)

Conversational pace where you can speak in full sentences. Heart rate typically 60-75% of maximum. Forms 80% of your training.

Zone 3 (Threshold)

Comfortably hard effort, sustainable for 20-60 minutes. You can speak in short phrases but not full conversations.

Brick Session

Bike-to-run workout with no break between disciplines. Teaches legs to adapt from cycling to running motion patterns.

Cadence

Steps per minute when running (target 170-180) or pedal RPM when cycling (target 85-100). Higher cadence generally improves efficiency.

Negative Split

Racing strategy where the second half is faster than the first half. Requires discipline early but usually results in better overall times.

Taper

Planned reduction in training volume (40-60%) while maintaining intensity, designed to arrive at race day fresh and rested.

T1/T2

Transition 1 (swim to bike) and Transition 2 (bike to run). Often called the 'fourth discipline' - efficiency here saves valuable time.

Sighting

Swimming technique of lifting eyes only (not full head) to check direction in open water. Do every 10-12 strokes.

Drafting

Swimming close behind another athlete's feet (30-50cm) to reduce energy expenditure by 20-30%. Legal in triathlon swimming.

FTP (Functional Threshold Power)

Maximum power you can sustain for one hour on the bike. Used to set cycling training zones and race pacing targets.

RPE (Rate of Perceived Exertion)

Subjective effort scale from 1-10. Useful when heart rate monitors aren't available or reliable.

80/20 Rule

Training principle where 80% of sessions are easy (Zone 1-2) and 20% are hard (Zone 3+). Maximizes adaptation while minimizing injury risk.

Active Recovery

Very easy movement (Zone 1) that promotes blood flow and recovery without adding training stress. Walking, easy swimming, or gentle cycling.

Polarized Training

Training approach that emphasizes very easy and very hard efforts while minimizing moderate intensities. Foundation of effective endurance training.

psychology Coach's Tips

pool

Master Open Water Skills

Practice sighting every 10-12 strokes and learn to draft 30-50cm behind other swimmers' feet. Open water swimming uses 15-20% more energy than pool swimming, so get specific practice before race day.

directions_bike

High Cadence Saves Your Run

Target 85-100 RPM on the bike to preserve your leg muscles for the run. Low cadence (70-75 RPM) burns more glycogen and creates more muscular fatigue that will hurt your run split.

directions_run

Run Cadence for Injury Prevention

Aim for 170-180 steps per minute with light, quick footsteps. Higher cadence reduces ground contact time and impact forces, lowering injury risk while improving efficiency.

restaurant

Start Fueling Early

For sessions over 90 minutes, begin taking carbs within the first 20-30 minutes. Don't wait until you feel tired - by then you're already behind on energy replacement.

psychology

Practice Race Day Mantras

Develop 2-3 positive phrases like 'Smooth and strong' or 'I've trained for this' and use them during hard training sessions. Mental skills must be practiced, not invented on race day.

schedule

Respect Recovery Weeks

Every 4th week should be 50% of your peak training volume. Your body adapts and gets stronger during recovery, not during the hard training itself. Don't skip these weeks.

favorite

Monitor Morning Heart Rate

Take your resting heart rate each morning before getting out of bed. A spike of 10+ BPM above normal may indicate you need an easy day instead of the planned hard session.

water_drop

Hydration Math

Drink 500-750ml per hour during moderate sessions, 750-1000ml per hour in hot conditions. Dehydration of just 2% bodyweight significantly impairs performance and increases perceived effort.
