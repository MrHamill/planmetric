menu_book How To Use This Plan

  • calendar_month Your training days are Tue, Wed, Thu, Fri, Sat. Each week follows the same day structure so you can build a consistent routine.
  • speed All sessions include target zones (HR, pace, or power) plus RPE so you can train by feel if needed. Stay in the prescribed zones — more is not better.
  • trending_up Volume builds progressively with recovery weeks every 3-4 weeks. Trust the process — the easy weeks are when adaptation happens.
  • expand_all Click any week to expand it and see your daily sessions. Each session includes warm-up, main set, cool-down, and a coaching note.
  • health_and_safety Listen to your body. If something hurts beyond normal training fatigue, take an extra rest day. Consistency over heroics.
  • swap_horiz Brick sessions (bike-to-run) simulate race conditions. Practise your transition routine and nutrition strategy every time.

timeline Your Training Phases

foundation

Base Building Weeks 1–4

Foundation phase focusing on aerobic development and technique refinement. All sessions are at comfortable intensities (Zone 1-2) to build your engine without fatigue. Perfect time to work on swimming technique and establish consistent training habits.

trending_up

Strength & Endurance Weeks 5–8

Progressive phase where we introduce race-specific intensities and longer sessions. You'll start practicing threshold efforts and race-pace work while maintaining your aerobic base. This is where fitness gains accelerate.

bolt

Peak Fitness Weeks 9–11

Highest intensity phase with race-simulation sessions and maximum training stress. Every session prepares you directly for race day demands. Quality becomes more important than quantity as we fine-tune your fitness.

self_improvement

Taper & Race Prep Weeks 12–12

Final preparation phase where volume drops significantly but intensity remains. Your fitness is already built - now we're ensuring you arrive at the start line fresh, sharp, and ready to perform at your best.

Phase 1: Foundation Build

Weeks 1–4

Week 1 — BASE
4.4h 13 Apr–19 2026
Welcome to your Olympic triathlon journey! This first week establishes your aerobic base with technique-focused sessions. All efforts should feel conversational and controlled - resist the urge to push harder. Focus on consistency and building good movement patterns across all three disciplines.

Tue - Swim Technique (6:00am–6:35am)

SWIM Z1-Z2 35min
Warm-up: 200m easy freestyle, focusing on long strokes and full extension
Main Set: 6 × 200m freestyle (30 sec rest between) - alternate 50m drill/50m swim. Drills: catch-up, single arm, high elbow catch. Focus on rotation and maintaining streamlined position. Pace: 2:20-2:30/100m
Cool-down: 200m easy freestyle, concentrate on smooth breathing pattern
Total: 1600m (35 min)
Coach's Note:
This is about building water feel and efficiency, not speed. Focus on one technical element per 200m - rotation, catch, or breathing rhythm. If you feel breathless, you're working too hard. Swimming is a skill sport - perfect practice makes perfect performance.

Wed - Bike Endurance (6:00am–6:35am)

BIKE Z1-Z2 35min
Warm-up: 8 min easy spinning at 85-95 RPM, gradually building to Zone 1
Main Set: 20 min steady Zone 2 at 85-100 RPM (should feel 'comfortably moderate'). Focus on smooth pedal stroke and maintaining higher cadence. Power should be around 135-150W if using power meter
Cool-down: 7 min easy spinning, gradually reducing intensity
Total: 35 min
Coach's Note:
Higher cadence is crucial for triathlon success - it saves your run legs! Most athletes naturally gravitate to 70-75 RPM, but 85-100 RPM is more cardiovascular than muscular. This feels weird initially but pays dividends on race day. Stay relaxed in your upper body and focus on smooth circles.

Thu - Easy Run (6:00am–6:35am)

RUN Z1-Z2 35min
Warm-up: 5 min walk/easy jog, building to comfortable Zone 1 pace
Main Set: 25 min continuous running at 6:30-7:00/km pace (fully conversational). Focus on 170-180 steps per minute cadence and landing with feet under your body, not ahead
Cool-down: 5 min walk with gentle stretching
Total: 35 min (~5.5km)
Coach's Note:
Easy means EASY - you should be able to chat comfortably throughout. This pace builds your aerobic engine without breaking you down. Count your steps for 15 seconds and multiply by 4 to check cadence. Landing with a higher cadence reduces injury risk and improves efficiency.

Fri - Long Ride (6:00am–7:25am)

BIKE Z1-Z2 85min
Warm-up: 10 min easy spinning, building to Zone 1-2 over the first 15 minutes
Main Set: 65 min steady Zone 2 riding at 85-95 RPM. Keep effort conversational but purposeful. Practice drinking every 15-20 minutes. Focus on smooth, circular pedaling action
Cool-down: 10 min easy spinning with focus on leg speed
Total: 85 min
Coach's Note:
This is your aerobic foundation session - the most important workout of your week. The effort should feel 'all day sustainable' and you should finish feeling like you could continue. Hydrate consistently (aim for 500-600ml/hour) and practice your race-day nutrition timing. Cadence work now prevents muscular overload later.

Sat - Long Run (7:00am–8:25am)

RUN Z1-Z2 85min
Warm-up: 10 min easy jog/walk, building gradually to comfortable pace
Main Set: 65 min continuous running at 6:15-6:45/km pace. Stay conversational throughout - break into walk if needed. Focus on relaxed shoulders, quick cadence, and rhythmic breathing
Cool-down: 10 min walk with gentle stretching
Total: 85 min (~13km)
Coach's Note:
Your most important session for race-day success. The pace should feel sustainable and controlled - think 'could I maintain this for another hour?' Walk breaks are perfectly acceptable if needed. This builds your aerobic base and teaches your body to burn fat efficiently. Remember: time on feet matters more than speed at this stage.
Week 2 — BASE
5.0h 20 Apr–26 2026
Building on week 1 with slightly increased duration. Continue focusing on technique and aerobic development. Your body is adapting to the training stimulus - some fatigue is normal, but you should still feel energetic for each session. Maintain the conversational pace discipline.

Tue - Swim Technique (6:00am–6:40am)

SWIM Z1-Z2 40min
Warm-up: 250m easy freestyle, focusing on bilateral breathing every 3 strokes
Main Set: 8 × 200m freestyle (30 sec rest) - 100m drill + 100m swim. Drills: fingertip drag, catch-up, high elbow. Swim portions at 2:15-2:25/100m pace, focusing on long strokes and rotation
Cool-down: 200m easy freestyle with emphasis on exhaling fully underwater
Total: 1850m (40 min)
Coach's Note:
You should be getting more comfortable with bilateral breathing and stroke rhythm. The drill portions teach your nervous system proper movement patterns, while the swim portions integrate those patterns at moderate intensity. Don't rush the stroke - distance per stroke is more important than stroke rate at this stage.

Wed - Bike Endurance (6:00am–6:40am)

BIKE Z1-Z2 40min
Warm-up: 10 min progressive build from Zone 1 to Zone 2, focusing on pedal smoothness
Main Set: 22 min steady Zone 2 at 85-100 RPM. Effort should feel 'comfortably moderate' - you could sustain this for hours. Practice shifting smoothly and maintaining cadence through gear changes
Cool-down: 8 min easy spinning with high leg speed (100+ RPM)
Total: 40 min
Coach's Note:
You're building aerobic capacity while training your neuromuscular system for efficient pedaling. Zone 2 should feel sustainable but purposeful - not easy, not hard. The high-cadence cooldown teaches your legs to move efficiently and prepares them for running. Focus on relaxed upper body and smooth breathing.

Thu - Easy Run (6:00am–6:40am)

RUN Z1-Z2 40min
Warm-up: 6 min walk/easy jog progression to comfortable running pace
Main Set: 28 min continuous running at 6:30-7:00/km pace. Focus on midfoot landing, quick cadence (170-180 spm), and controlled breathing. Should feel conversational throughout
Cool-down: 6 min walk with focus on posture and relaxation
Total: 40 min (~6km)
Coach's Note:
Your running legs are developing! This slightly longer duration teaches your body to maintain good form for extended periods. If you feel like pushing harder, resist - these easy runs are building your aerobic base and preparing your body for the harder work to come. Good form now prevents injuries later.

Fri - Long Ride (6:00am–7:30am)

BIKE Z1-Z2 90min
Warm-up: 12 min easy spinning, building gradually to Zone 2 over first 20 minutes
Main Set: 65 min steady Zone 2 riding. Practice race-day hydration (150-200ml every 15-20 min) and comfortable gear selection. Focus on maintaining 85-95 RPM even on gentle climbs
Cool-down: 13 min easy spinning, finishing with 3 min at 100+ RPM
Total: 90 min
Coach's Note:
This extended time in Zone 2 is building your aerobic engine and teaching metabolic efficiency. You should finish feeling pleasantly tired but not exhausted. Hydration practice is crucial - dehydration by even 2% significantly impacts performance. The high-cadence finish prepares your legs for the transition to running.

Sat - Long Run (7:00am–8:35am)

RUN Z1-Z2 95min
Warm-up: 12 min progressive walk to jog to comfortable running pace
Main Set: 70 min continuous running at 6:15-6:45/km pace. Stay conversational and relaxed. Walk for 1-2 minutes if needed, but aim to run continuously. Focus on efficient form and rhythmic breathing
Cool-down: 13 min walk with gentle movement to maintain circulation
Total: 95 min (~14.5km)
Coach's Note:
You're building endurance and teaching your body to use fat as fuel efficiently. The slightly increased duration should feel manageable - this is progressive overload in action. Focus on staying relaxed, especially in your shoulders and hands. This session sets you up for success in longer efforts to come.
Week 3 — BASE
5.8h 27 Apr–3 May 2026
Peak base-building week with the longest duration sessions so far. Your aerobic fitness is developing nicely. Continue emphasizing technique and maintaining conversational efforts. This volume prepares your body for the training intensification coming in the build phase.

Tue - Swim Technique (6:00am–6:45am)

SWIM Z1-Z2 45min
Warm-up: 300m easy freestyle, mixing bilateral breathing patterns (3-5-3-5 strokes)
Main Set: 10 × 200m freestyle (30 sec rest) - 50m drill/150m swim. Drills: sculling, catch-up, single arm. Swim portions at 2:10-2:20/100m, focusing on maintaining stroke length as you get tired
Cool-down: 250m easy freestyle with focus on relaxed, rhythmic swimming
Total: 2250m (45 min)
Coach's Note:
This is your longest swim session in the base phase, building both fitness and stroke durability. As you fatigue, resist shortening your stroke - maintain distance per stroke even if stroke rate slows slightly. The varied breathing patterns improve your race-day flexibility and oxygen efficiency.

Wed - Bike Endurance (6:00am–6:45am)

BIKE Z1-Z2 45min
Warm-up: 12 min progressive build from easy spinning to solid Zone 2
Main Set: 25 min steady Zone 2 at 85-100 RPM. This should feel like a pace you could maintain for 3+ hours. Practice drinking and eating (if using nutrition) while maintaining smooth pedaling
Cool-down: 8 min easy spinning with focus on leg speed and efficiency
Total: 45 min
Coach's Note:
You're developing the metabolic efficiency that separates good triathletes from great ones. Zone 2 teaches your body to burn fat efficiently while sparing precious glycogen stores. The longer duration at this intensity is building your aerobic base and improving your body's ability to clear lactate. Stay patient with the process - this foundation is crucial.

Thu - Easy Run (6:00am–6:45am)

RUN Z1-Z2 45min
Warm-up: 7 min walk/jog progression, focusing on gradual warming of muscles
Main Set: 30 min continuous running at 6:30-7:00/km pace. Maintain conversational effort throughout. Focus on landing efficiently and keeping your cadence around 170-180 spm
Cool-down: 8 min walk with attention to posture and breathing
Total: 45 min (~6.5km)
Coach's Note:
Your longest easy run yet - this builds running-specific endurance and teaches your body to maintain good form when fatigued. The pace should feel controlled and sustainable. If you're breathing hard, slow down. These easy runs are investments in your future speed and race-day durability.

Fri - Long Ride (6:00am–7:30am)

BIKE Z1-Z2 90min
Warm-up: 15 min progressive warm-up, building to comfortable Zone 2 over first 20 minutes
Main Set: 60 min steady Zone 2 effort. Practice race-day position, hydration schedule (aim for 600-700ml total), and gear selection. Maintain 85-95 RPM throughout
Cool-down: 15 min easy spinning, finishing with 5 min at higher leg speed
Total: 90 min
Coach's Note:
This sustained Zone 2 effort is building your aerobic engine and metabolic efficiency. You should feel like you're working but could continue for much longer. Practice everything you'll need on race day - hydration timing, comfortable position, and smooth gear changes. Hydrate consistently throughout this session as you're losing significant fluid through sweat.

Sat - Long Run (7:00am–8:50am)

RUN Z1-Z2 110min
Warm-up: 15 min progressive build from walk to comfortable running pace
Main Set: 80 min continuous running at 6:15-6:45/km pace. This is your longest run yet - focus on staying relaxed and conversational. Walk briefly if needed, but aim for continuous running
Cool-down: 15 min walk with gentle movement and focus on recovery
Total: 110 min (~16.5km)
Coach's Note:
This is a significant milestone in your endurance development. The extended duration teaches your body to burn fat efficiently and builds the mental toughness needed for race day. Stay patient with the pace - it should feel sustainable throughout. Hydrate well before and after this session, and consider carrying water if it's warm. You're building the foundation for race-day success.
Week 4 — BASE (Recovery)
4.4h 4 May–10 2026
Recovery week to absorb the training adaptations from the past three weeks. Reduced volume allows your body to rebuild stronger while maintaining fitness. Use this week to focus on technique refinement and mental preparation for the upcoming build phase.

Tue - Swim Technique (6:00am–6:35am)

SWIM Z1-Z2 35min
Warm-up: 200m easy freestyle with focus on relaxation and flow
Main Set: 6 × 200m freestyle (45 sec rest) - emphasis on perfect technique over speed. Alternate 100m drill/100m easy swim. Focus on catch, rotation, and streamlining. Pace: 2:25-2:35/100m
Cool-down: 200m very easy freestyle, focusing on long, relaxed strokes
Total: 1600m (35 min)
Coach's Note:
Recovery week swimming is about technique refinement and maintaining feel for the water. The longer rest periods allow you to focus on perfect form without fatigue compromising your stroke. This is an investment week - your body is absorbing the adaptations from the past three weeks while staying ready for increased intensity ahead.

Wed - Bike Endurance (6:00am–6:35am)

BIKE Z1-Z2 35min
Warm-up: 10 min very easy spinning, focusing on smooth pedal stroke
Main Set: 18 min comfortable Zone 1-2 riding at 85-100 RPM. Should feel easy and restorative. Focus on pedal smoothness and maintaining higher cadence without strain
Cool-down: 7 min easy spinning with emphasis on leg speed
Total: 35 min
Coach's Note:
This should feel easy and refreshing - if you're working hard, you're defeating the purpose of recovery week. Your body is rebuilding stronger during this reduced volume. Use this time to work on technical elements like smooth shifting, efficient position, and pedal stroke mechanics without the stress of longer duration or higher intensity.

Thu - Easy Run (6:00am–6:35am)

RUN Z1-Z2 35min
Warm-up: 5 min walk/very easy jog progression
Main Set: 25 min easy running at 6:30-7:00/km pace. Should feel effortless and restorative. Focus on form cues: quick cadence, midfoot landing, relaxed shoulders
Cool-down: 5 min walk with gentle stretching
Total: 35 min (~5.5km)
Coach's Note:
This run should feel like active recovery - easy, refreshing, and energizing rather than depleting. Your body is supercompensating from the training load of the past three weeks. Focus on running mechanics and enjoy the feeling of easy, flowing movement. You should finish feeling better than when you started.

Fri - Long Ride (6:00am–7:25am)

BIKE Z1-Z2 85min
Warm-up: 12 min easy spinning with focus on warming gradually
Main Set: 60 min comfortable Zone 1-2 riding. Should feel sustainable and pleasant. Practice skills like drinking, shifting smoothly, and maintaining position without strain
Cool-down: 13 min easy spinning, finishing relaxed
Total: 85 min
Coach's Note:
Even though this is 'recovery week,' this session still provides valuable aerobic stimulus while allowing adaptation. The effort should feel sustainable and pleasant - think 'I could do this all day.' Use this time to practice technical skills and maintain your aerobic base. Hydrate normally but don't stress about nutrition for this duration.

Sat - Long Run (7:00am–8:25am)

RUN Z1-Z2 85min
Warm-up: 10 min progressive walk to easy jog to comfortable pace
Main Set: 65 min easy running at 6:15-6:45/km pace. Focus on staying relaxed and enjoying the movement. Should feel restorative rather than challenging
Cool-down: 10 min walk with attention to how your body feels
Total: 85 min (~13km)
Coach's Note:
This run maintains your aerobic base while giving your body a break from the stress of longer sessions. The effort should feel conversational and sustainable - resist any urges to push harder. Your body is adapting and getting stronger during this recovery period. Focus on good form and enjoy the meditative aspects of easy running.

Phase 2: Aerobic Development

Weeks 5–8

Week 5 — BUILD
6.4h 11 May–17 2026
Welcome to the build phase! We're introducing race-specific intensities while maintaining your aerobic base. The brick session is your first taste of bike-to-run transition stress. Listen to your body as training stress increases, and maintain discipline with intensity - hard days hard, easy days easy.

Tue - Swim Technique (6:00am–6:45am)

SWIM Z1-Z2 45min
Warm-up: 300m easy freestyle, building stroke rate gradually over the distance
Main Set: 10 × 200m freestyle (30 sec rest) - 50m build/150m steady. Build portions: start easy, finish at threshold effort (~2:00/100m). Steady portions at comfortable pace (2:15-2:25/100m)
Cool-down: 250m easy freestyle with focus on long, relaxed strokes
Total: 2250m (45 min)
Coach's Note:
You're starting to taste race-pace efforts! The build 50s teach you to accelerate smoothly while the steady portions develop pace sustainment. This is your first introduction to swimming at threshold intensity - it should feel 'comfortably hard' but controlled. Focus on maintaining good technique even as effort increases.

Wed - Brick Session (6:00am–7:25am)

BRICK Z2-Z3 85min
Warm-up: Bike: 15 min progressive warm-up to Zone 2. Run: None (immediate transition)
Main Set: Bike: 55 min alternating 10 min Zone 2 / 5 min Zone 3 (3 × each). Zone 3 should feel 'comfortably hard' at race effort. Immediate transition to: Run: 15 min at target race pace (5:30-5:45/km), no walking
Cool-down: 10 min easy walk focusing on how your legs feel post-transition
Total: 85 min
Coach's Note:
Welcome to your first brick! The bike portion introduces race-specific Zone 3 efforts while the immediate run teaches your legs to adapt to the bike-run transition. Your legs will feel heavy initially on the run - this is normal and improves with practice. The Zone 3 bike efforts should feel sustainable but purposeful. This session is crucial preparation for race day success.

Thu - Easy Run (6:00am–6:45am)

RUN Z1-Z2 45min
Warm-up: 7 min walk/jog, paying attention to how your legs feel after yesterday's brick
Main Set: 30 min easy running at 6:30-7:00/km pace. This is active recovery from the brick session. Stay conversational and focus on smooth, relaxed form
Cool-down: 8 min walk with gentle stretching, especially calves and hip flexors
Total: 45 min (~6.5km)
Coach's Note:
This easy run is crucial for recovery from your first brick session. Your legs might feel different after yesterday's bike-run combination - this is normal and part of the adaptation process. Keep the effort truly easy and focus on maintaining good form. This recovery run helps flush metabolic waste and maintains movement quality.

Fri - Long Ride (6:00am–7:30am)

BIKE Z1-Z2 90min
Warm-up: 15 min progressive build to comfortable Zone 2 intensity
Main Set: 60 min steady Zone 2 effort at 85-95 RPM. Focus on race position, smooth pedaling, and consistent hydration. Should feel like a pace you could maintain for 3+ hours
Cool-down: 15 min easy spinning, gradually reducing intensity
Total: 90 min
Coach's Note:
This aerobic base session balances the week's increased intensity. Zone 2 should feel sustainable and controlled - you're building the aerobic engine that supports your race-pace efforts. Maintain higher cadence to spare your run legs and practice race-day skills like hydration and position. Hydrate consistently throughout this session (aim for 600-750ml total).

Sat - Long Run (7:00am–8:45am)

RUN Z1-Z2 105min
Warm-up: 12 min progressive walk to jog to comfortable running pace
Main Set: 80 min running at 6:15-6:30/km pace - slightly faster than previous weeks but still conversational. Focus on maintaining good form and efficient movement patterns
Cool-down: 13 min walk with attention to recovery and how your body feels
Total: 105 min (~16km)
Coach's Note:
This run builds on your base phase fitness while introducing a slightly more purposeful pace. You should still be able to hold conversation, but the effort feels more 'intentional' than previous easy runs. This bridges the gap between base aerobic development and race-specific fitness. Stay disciplined with the pace - faster isn't always better.
Week 6 — BUILD
6.8h 18 May–24 2026
Building steady aerobic capacity with quality brick sessions. Focus on smooth transitions and maintaining form as duration increases.

Tue - Swim Technique (6:00am–6:45am)

SWIM Z1-Z2 45min
Warm-up: 200m easy freestyle, 4×50m build (25m easy/25m faster)
Main Set: 6×100m CSS+8-12 sec with 20 sec rest, 200m pull buoy easy, 6×50m catch-up drill with 15 sec rest, 200m steady
Cool-down: 200m easy choice stroke, focus on long strokes
Total: 1,800m (45 min)
Coach's Note:
Prioritise stroke length over speed today. During the catch-up drill, really focus on the high elbow catch position - this single technique improvement will save you more time than any fitness gain. Keep the main set aerobic and conversational if you could speak.

Wed - Brick Session (6:00am–7:30am)

BRICK Z2-Z3 90min
Warm-up: 10 min easy spin, build cadence to 90+ RPM
Main Set: 60 min ride at Z2 (75% FTP), maintain 85-95 RPM throughout → immediate transition to 20 min run at Z2-Z3 pace (10-15 sec/km faster than easy pace)
Cool-down: 5 min easy walk
Total: 95 min (bike 70 min + run 20 min + 5 min cool)
Coach's Note:
This is where triathlon fitness is built - the ability to run well off the bike. Keep the bike portion controlled and focus on high cadence to preserve your legs. In T2, expect heavy legs for the first 5 minutes of running - this is normal and will pass. Practice your race-day transition routine.

Thu - Easy Run (6:00am–6:45am)

RUN Z1-Z2 45min
Warm-up: 5 min walk, 5 min easy jog
Main Set: 30 min at easy conversational pace (6:00/km), focus on 170-180 spm cadence
Cool-down: 5 min walk, light stretching
Total: 45 min
Coach's Note:
This should feel genuinely easy - you should be able to hold a full conversation throughout. If you're breathing hard, slow down. These recovery runs are crucial for building your aerobic base while allowing your body to adapt to training stress.

Fri - Long Ride (6:00am–7:30am)

BIKE Z1-Z2 90min
Warm-up: 15 min gradual build from easy to Z2
Main Set: 60 min steady Z2 (65-75% FTP), maintain 85-95 RPM, practice nutrition every 20 min
Cool-down: 15 min easy spin
Total: 90 min
Coach's Note:
This is your aerobic engine development session. Stay disciplined with the power/effort - it should feel like you could continue for another hour. Hydrate 500-750ml per hour and practice your race-day fueling strategy. Higher cadence will help preserve your legs for tomorrow's run.

Sat - Long Run (7:00am–8:55am)

RUN Z1-Z2 115min
Warm-up: 10 min easy jog, building gradually
Main Set: 90 min at conversational Z1-Z2 pace (6:00-6:15/km), walk through aid stations every 20 min
Cool-down: 15 min walk, stretching
Total: 115 min (~19km)
Coach's Note:
Your most important session of the week for building endurance. Start conservatively and maintain steady effort throughout - resist the urge to pick up the pace in the middle. Practice race-day fueling every 30-35 minutes with whatever you plan to use on race day. This builds both physical and mental endurance for race day.
Week 7 — BUILD
7.2h 25 May–31 2026
Peak build volume with extended sessions. Focus on race-pace efforts and maintaining good form under fatigue.

Tue - Swim Technique (6:00am–6:50am)

SWIM Z1-Z2 50min
Warm-up: 300m easy freestyle, 4×50m build (25m easy/25m fast)
Main Set: 8×100m CSS+5-10 sec with 15 sec rest, 300m pull buoy steady, 8×50m single-arm drill alternating arms with 20 sec rest, 4×100m descending (get faster each 100m)
Cool-down: 200m easy backstroke or easy freestyle
Total: 2,100m (50 min)
Coach's Note:
Stepping up the volume and intensity slightly. The descending 400m at the end teaches you to finish strong when tired - crucial for race day. Focus on maintaining stroke technique even as you get faster in the final set. Your stroke should feel smooth and controlled, never forced.

Wed - Brick Session (6:00am–7:30am)

BRICK Z2-Z3 90min
Warm-up: 10 min easy, building to Z2
Main Set: 60 min ride with 3×8 min at Z3 (80-85% FTP) with 4 min Z2 recovery between → immediate T2 to 20 min run starting Z2, building to Z3 in final 5 min
Cool-down: 5 min easy walk
Total: 95 min (bike 70 min + run 20 min + 5 min cool)
Coach's Note:
Adding some race-intensity work to your brick training. The Z3 intervals on the bike simulate race effort - this should feel comfortably hard but sustainable. Focus on quick, smooth transitions and expect the run to feel challenging initially. This session builds your ability to sustain race pace when already fatigued.

Thu - Easy Run (6:00am–6:50am)

RUN Z1-Z2 50min
Warm-up: 5 min walk, 5 min easy jog
Main Set: 35 min at easy conversational pace (6:00/km), include 4×20 sec strides at end
Cool-down: 5 min walk, light stretching
Total: 50 min
Coach's Note:
Easy pace with a touch of speed to keep your legs fresh. The strides should feel smooth and controlled - not an all-out sprint. These short accelerations help maintain your neuromuscular coordination without adding significant fatigue. Focus on good running form throughout.

Fri - Long Ride (6:00am–7:30am)

BIKE Z1-Z2 90min
Warm-up: 15 min gradual build to Z2
Main Set: 60 min steady Z2 with final 15 min at race effort (Z2-Z3), maintain high cadence throughout
Cool-down: 15 min easy recovery spin
Total: 90 min
Coach's Note:
Finishing with race-effort pace to simulate late-race conditions. This teaches you to lift your effort when your legs are already tired - a key skill for triathlon. Hydrate consistently and practice your pacing discipline. The final 15 minutes should feel like your target race effort.

Sat - Long Run (7:00am–9:00am)

Pre-Race Timeline
4-5 hours before: High-carb breakfast (1-2g/kg bodyweight). Examples: porridge with banana and honey, or toast with jam. Avoid fiber and unfamiliar foods.
3-4 hours before: Begin hydrating with 500-750ml fluid. Include sodium if it's a hot day.
2 hours before: Arrive at venue, collect race pack, set up transition area methodically. Visualize your race plan.
90 min before: Light warm-up swim if permitted (200-300m easy). Practice sighting and get comfortable with water conditions.
45 min before: Final gear check, apply sunscreen, use bathroom. Stay relaxed and focused.
30 min before: Optional energy gel with 150ml water if race is longer than 60 min. Move to swim start area.
15 min before: Dynamic warm-up, final mental preparation. Remind yourself: 'I've trained for this.'
Your Race Strategy

Swim (1500m - 25-35 min)

Start conservatively for first 200m, settle into rhythm. Sight every 10-12 strokes using crocodile technique. Find feet to draft behind if possible - saves 20-30% energy. If panicking, roll to backstroke briefly to recover composure.

T1 (Target: 2-3 min)

Jog to bike, remove wetsuit to waist while moving. Put on helmet BEFORE touching bike. Mount only after mount line.

Bike (40km - 60-90 min)

First 10km: settle in, don't chase early leaders. Maintain 85-95 RPM cadence. Fuel every 20 min with 20-30g carbs. Save energy for the run - bike sets up everything.

T2 (Target: 1-2 min)

Rack bike properly, helmet off, running shoes on. Take 10 seconds to compose yourself before running.

Run (10km - 45-70 min)

First 2km will feel awkward - normal brick sensation. Settle into race pace by 3km. Focus on quick cadence (180 spm). Push hard only in final 2km.

Mental Strategy

Chunking Strategy

Break race into 6 manageable pieces: swim to first buoy, complete swim, T1, bike first half, bike second half, T2, run first 5km, run second 5km. Celebrate completing each chunk.

Self-Talk Mantras

Swim: 'Smooth and strong' | Bike: 'Steady power, save the legs' | Run: 'Quick and light, I've got this'

Managing Adversity

If something goes wrong: 'Yes, and...' technique. 'Yes, that was challenging, and I still have plenty of race left.' Focus on what you can control right now.

Confidence Anchors

Remember your best training sessions. Recall how strong you felt during peak week. Trust that your body knows what to do - you've rehearsed this many times.

school Glossary

CSS (Critical Swim Speed)

Your threshold pace for swimming, calculated as (400m time - 200m time) ÷ 2. This becomes your baseline for setting swim training zones.

FTP (Functional Threshold Power)

The highest power you can sustain for one hour on the bike. Used to set training zones and pacing targets.

Brick Session

Bike-to-run training where you transition immediately from cycling to running with no break, simulating race conditions.

Zone 1-2 (Aerobic Base)

Easy, conversational pace where you can speak in full sentences. Builds aerobic fitness with minimal fatigue.

Zone 3-4 (Threshold)

Comfortably hard effort around your race pace. You can speak only a few words at a time.

RPM (Revolutions Per Minute)

Bike cadence measurement. Optimal range is 85-100 RPM for triathlon to preserve running legs.

Sighting

Swimming technique of lifting your eyes (not whole head) to navigate in open water. Practice every 10-12 strokes.

Negative Split

Racing strategy where the second half is faster than the first half. Requires discipline early in the race.

T1/T2

Transition areas where you change from swim to bike (T1) and bike to run (T2). Speed here can significantly impact overall time.

Drafting

Swimming or cycling close behind another athlete to reduce energy expenditure. Legal in swim, illegal in most age-group bike segments.

Build Intervals

Training sets where pace gradually increases within each repetition, developing speed and pacing awareness.

Descending Set

Training intervals where each repetition gets progressively faster, building speed and race finishing ability.

Strides

Short 15-20 second accelerations during easy runs, maintaining neuromuscular speed without creating fatigue.

Wetsuit Legal

Water temperature where wetsuits are permitted (typically 14-22°C). Provides buoyancy and speed advantages.

Crocodile Sighting

Swimming technique where only eyes emerge from water to sight, minimizing disruption to stroke rhythm and body position.

pool

Master Your Sighting

Practice sighting every 6th stroke in the pool. In open water, use the crocodile technique - eyes only, not your whole head. This keeps you on course while maintaining speed.

directions_bike

Higher Cadence = Better Run

Aim for 85-95 RPM on the bike. Higher cadence uses more cardiovascular fitness and less muscular power, leaving your legs fresher for the run leg.

psychology

Break Your Race Into Chunks

Don't think about the whole race distance. Break it into manageable pieces: swim to buoy, T1, bike first half, etc. Celebrate completing each chunk.

sports_bar

Fuel Early and Often

Start taking in carbs within the first 20-30 minutes of any session over 60 minutes. Your body can absorb 30-60g per hour - train your gut to handle race-day nutrition.

favorite

Easy Days Must Be Easy

80% of your training should be at conversational pace. If you can't speak in full sentences, you're going too hard on easy days.

swap_horiz

Practice Transitions

T1 and T2 are the 'fourth discipline.' Practice your routine: wetsuit removal, helmet on before touching bike, smooth shoe changes. Every second counts.

thermostat

Know Your Heart Rate Zones

Running HR is your baseline. Subtract 5-10 BPM for cycling zones, and 10-15 BPM for swimming zones. The same effort feels different in each sport.

schedule

Recovery Is When You Get Stronger

Take recovery weeks every 4th week at 50% normal volume. Your body adapts during rest, not during the hard training sessions.
