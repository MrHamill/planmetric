## Post 1 — Monday 14 Apr | EDU | Reel

**Concept:** "Your easy pace should feel embarrassingly slow"
Text overlay on a sunrise or golden hour clip of you or a mate jogging at a genuinely easy pace. Slow, relaxed, almost looks lazy. The contrast between the epic backdrop and the chill effort IS the point.

**Filming note:** Sunrise session. Wide shot from behind — runner silhouetted against the sky, clearly not racing. Side angle showing relaxed face, mouth closed, no strain. Bonus: someone walking a dog passes them (plays into the brand voice line).

**Text on screen (timed to appear):**
1. "80% of your training should look like this"
2. "Not this" [cut to a clip of hard running / gasping]
3. "Zone 2. Conversational pace. Embarrassingly slow."
4. "That's where your aerobic base is built."

**Caption:**
If you're not getting overtaken by someone walking a labrador, you're probably going too hard on your easy days.

Research across elite endurance athletes is clear: 80–90% of training should be at low intensity. Fully conversational. Below threshold.

Most age-groupers invert this. They go too hard on easy days and too easy on hard days. The result? Chronic fatigue, plateau, and wondering why the PB isn't coming.

Slow down to speed up. It works.

Save this for your next "easy" run that isn't actually easy.

**Concept:** "5 signs you're overtraining"
Educational carousel — 7 slides. This is high-save, high-share content. Works as text-on-image slides with your moody backdrop footage as still frames behind the text.

**Filming note:** Pull still frames from your best sunset/rain/golden hour clips. Each slide uses a different still as the background with semi-transparent dark overlay and white text on top. Consistent layout across all slides. Last slide = CTA.

**Slides:**

**Slide 1 (Hook):**
"5 signs you're overtraining
(and think you're just being tough)"

**Slide 2:**
"1. Your easy pace keeps getting slower"
Subtext: If your Zone 2 heart rate drifts higher every week at the same pace, your body is accumulating fatigue — not adapting.

**Slide 3:**
"2. You dread sessions you used to enjoy"
Subtext: Motivation dips are normal. But consistently dreading training is a signal, not a weakness.

**Slide 4:**
"3. Niggles that won't go away"
Subtext: That knee twinge on every run? Your body is telling you something. Pushing through is not toughness — it's denial.

**Slide 5:**
"4. You're getting sick more often"
Subtext: Heavy training suppresses immune function. If you're catching every cold going around, your load is too high.

**Slide 6:**
"5. PBs have flatlined or gone backwards"
Subtext: More training doesn't always mean more fitness. Sometimes the answer is a recovery week, not another interval session.

**Slide 7 (CTA):**
"The fix? Train smarter, not harder.
PlanMetric builds plans with recovery weeks built in — because that's where adaptation actually happens."
planmetric.com.au

**Caption:**
Overtraining doesn't look like collapse. It looks like slow decline — slightly worse paces, slightly more tired, slightly less motivated. Until one day you realise you've been going backwards for months.

The 10% rule exists for a reason. So do recovery weeks.

If any of these hit home, save this and share it with your training partner.
