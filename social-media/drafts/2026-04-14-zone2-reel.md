## Filming Instructions

### Shot List

| # | Duration | Shot | Details |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 0–3s | **HOOK — close-up of watch** | Film your watch/wrist mid-run showing an "embarrassingly slow" pace (e.g. 6:30–7:00/km). Camera tight on the watch face. This is the scroll-stopper. |
| 2 | 3–8s | **Wide shot — you running easy** | Film from the side on a path/trail. Visibly relaxed. Shoulders down, mouth closed, breathing through nose. Maybe someone walking a dog passes you at roughly the same speed. |
| 3 | 8–14s | **Talking head (or text overlay)** | Face camera. Say: "If you can't hold a full conversation at this pace, you're going too hard. Zone 2 should feel almost too easy." |

| 5 | 22–30s | **Running footage with text overlays** | Relaxed running B-roll. Stack text overlays: "Builds aerobic base" → "Burns fat as fuel" → "Prevents burnout" → "Makes race pace feel easier" (each on screen ~2s) |
| 6 | 30–38s | **Talking head close** | "80% of your training should be at this pace. The hard days earn it — but easy days build the engine." |
| 7 | 38–42s | **CTA card / logo** | Text overlay: "Save this for your next easy run" + @planmetric |

### Camera & Angles

- **Watch shot:** Use your phone in one hand, film your other wrist. Steady, no shake. Natural light.
- **Running wide:** Set phone on a tripod or lean it against something stable at waist height. Run past camera left to right. Morning golden hour light is ideal.
- **Talking head:** Chest-up framing. Outdoors, natural background (park, trail). Look at camera lens, not screen.
- **B-roll:** Handheld is fine — slight movement adds energy. Keep clips 2–3 seconds max.

### Text Overlays (in order of appearance)

1. **0–3s:** "Your easy pace should feel embarrassingly slow" (large, centred, bold)
2. **8–14s:** "Can you hold a conversation?" (smaller, lower third)

4. **22–30s:** Benefits list (stacked, appearing one by one)
5. **30–38s:** "80% easy. 20% hard." (bold statement)

## Caption

Your easy pace should feel embarrassingly slow.

If someone walking a labrador is keeping up with you — good. That's the point.

Zone 2 training builds your aerobic engine. It's where your body learns to burn fat, deliver oxygen efficiently, and actually recover between hard sessions.

80% of your weekly running should be at this effort. Not 50%. Not "most of it." Eighty percent.

The athletes who get this right? They smash race day. The ones who don't? They're cooked by week 6.

Save this. Check it before your next easy run.
