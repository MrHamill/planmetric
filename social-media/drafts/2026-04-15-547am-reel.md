## Filming Instructions

### Shot List

| # | Duration | Shot | Details |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 0–2s | **HOOK — black screen, alarm sound** | Film your phone alarm going off at 5:47am. Dark room, screen glow on your face. Raw and real — don't tidy up. |
| 2 | 2–5s | **Getting out of bed** | Side angle of you swinging legs out of bed in the dark. Slow, reluctant movement. Everyone relates to this. |
| 3 | 5–8s | **Lacing up shoes** | Close-up of hands tying running shoes. Kitchen bench or hallway floor. Low light, just a lamp or early dawn. |
| 4 | 8–11s | **Opening the front door** | Film from inside looking out. Dark street, maybe a streetlight. The moment of commitment. |
| 5 | 11–18s | **Running in early morning light** | Multiple quick cuts (2s each): empty streets, breath visible if cold, sunrise starting, footsteps on pavement. This is the beauty section. |
| 6 | 18–22s | **Watch/finish shot** | Stopping your watch after the run. Heavy breathing. Maybe a quick smile or nod — you did the thing. |
| 7 | 22–28s | **Post-run moment** | Coffee in hand, sun now up, sitting on front step or at kitchen bench. Calm. Satisfied. The payoff. |

### Camera & Angles

- **Alarm/bed shots:** Phone propped on bedside table filming you. Low light is the point — don't add lighting.
- **Shoe lacing:** Place phone on the floor angled up. Close crop, just hands and shoes.
- **Running B-roll:** Mix of tripod wide shots (run through frame) and handheld POV (phone in hand, path ahead). Dawn light does all the work.
- **Post-run:** Tripod or propped phone. Relaxed framing, chest up.

### Text Overlays (in order of appearance)

1. **0–2s:** "Nobody's watching at 5:47am" (large, centred, white on dark)
2. **5–8s:** "No likes. No applause." (fade in, smaller)
3. **11–18s:** "Just you and the road." (during running montage)
4. **18–22s:** "This is where it's built." (bold, on the watch stop moment)
5. **22–28s:** "Show up before the results do." (final frame, hold for 3s)

## Caption

Nobody's watching at 5:47am.

No likes. No finish line photos. No one cheering.

Just you, cold air, and a decision you made before your brain could talk you out of it.

Consistency isn't glamorous. It's dark mornings and tired legs and choosing to go anyway. But every race day PB, every "I can't believe I just did that" moment — it started here.

Not on race day. Not in the last week. Here. In the quiet.
