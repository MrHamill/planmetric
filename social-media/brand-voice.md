# PlanMetric Brand Voice

## Who We Are

PlanMetric builds personalised endurance training plans — running, triathlon, and cycling. Not cookie-cutter templates. Every plan is built from the athlete's biology, schedule, injury history, and goals. Plans run from purchase date to race date, never preset lengths.

## Who We Talk To

- Runners (5K to marathon)
- Triathletes (sprint to Ironman)
- Cyclists (road, gran fondo, time trials)
- All levels — first-timers to age-group competitors
- Mostly Australian, but global audience growing
- Age range: 25-55
- People who take their training seriously but don't take themselves too seriously

## Voice Attributes

### We Sound Like
- Your knowledgeable mate at the coffee shop after a long run
- A coach who respects your time and intelligence
- Someone who's been through the pain cave and can laugh about it

### We Don't Sound Like
- A university lecture
- A hype beast influencer ("LETS GOOO")
- A corporate fitness brand
- A gatekeeping elitist

## Language Rules

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| Use Aussie English (favour, personalised, colour) | Use American spelling |
| Say "smashed it", "nailed it", "cooked" | Say "crushed it", "killed it" |
| Use "your" and "you" — talk directly to the reader | Use "one should" or passive voice |
| Keep sentences short and punchy | Write long compound sentences |
| Use real numbers and specifics | Be vague ("improve your running") |
| Be self-deprecating about suffering | Be toxic-positive ("every run is amazing!") |

## Emoji Usage

- Minimal. 1-3 per caption max
- Never start a caption with an emoji
- Use for visual breaks in longer captions, not decoration
- Preferred: running related emojis, not generic fitness ones

## CTA Bank

Rotate these. Never use the same CTA twice in a row:

- "Link in bio to get started"
- "Save this for race week"
- "Tag your training partner who needs this"
- "Drop your goal race below"
- "DM us 'PLAN' for details"
- "What's your PB? Tell us below"
- "Share this with someone who's about to start training"
- "Follow for more that actually helps your training"
